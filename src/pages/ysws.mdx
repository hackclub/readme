The main thing in Hack Club are **You Ship, We ship (YSWS) programs**. 
In YSWS events, you maintain total ownership over the projects you submit. 
Time that was accrewed in Hackatime before a YSWS started also cannot be submitted.
Another thing to note: time spent on a project submitted to one YSWS event cannot be submitted to another YSWS event (no double-dipping).

You can find a list of active YSWS events on https://ysws.hackclub.com

## Philosophy
The philosophy behind YSWS events is this: to incentivize students to practice and make silly projects.
So reading documentation or watching a YouTube video don't count as hours but the hours spent building something using the knowledge gained from reading documentation or watching that YouTube video count.
The idea is to get hours of practice in by building and debugging.

Deven wrote a short blog post about how Hack Club approaches YSWS and how it determines funding titled [The plan for hack club world dominance](https://notes.deven.dev/posts/the-plan-for-hack-club-world-dominance) if you're interested and want to read it.


## YSWS
 YSWS programs come in all shapes and sizes. Some are short term (1-3 months). Some have no ending date (like [Boba](https://boba.hackclub.com/)!). Some only accept projects around a theme *(like the [Woof](https://woof.hackclub.com/)  YSWS that ended that only accepted dog themed projects)* but others accept any project no matter the theme like [Flavortown](https://flavortown.hackclub.com/)! 
 But they all give out prizes for the hours spent making a project around a given theme. You can nab yourself a 3d printer or credits for cloud providers or a Framework 16 laptop! 
 Some programs give out **grants** instead of physical prizes. For physical prizes: If you're outside of the United States, additional fees for customs/imports may apply. :(

## How to participate
To participate in YSWS programs, you have to verify you're a teenager by providing one of the following:
- A government-issued ID (ex. Passport, NIC), or
- A student ID & report card/transcript (only in certain countries: US, CA, AU, SG)

## How time is tracked
[Hackatime](https://hackatime.hackclub.com) (Hack Club's own wakatime instance) is used to track the time spent coding in IDEs like VSCode and Sublime and even Unity.
All you have to do is install the Wakatime plugin/extention for your IDE, connect it to Hackatime and you're good to go!
Wakatime tracks which files were opened, how many lines were added or deleted and when you switch files and reports that to Hackatime.

[Lapse](https://lapse.hackclub.com) is an alternative to Hackatime where you can record a video of you working (either record your desktop or your actual camera) and sync that time to Hackatime so that you can receive credit for it. 

Both Lapse and Hackatime can be used for tracking time spent on personal projects too!

# In-person Hackathons 
Some YSWS programs also host hackathons where teens can fly out to a city to build cool projects for fun and for prizes. 
Some hackathons have limited seats. 
Waviers are sent via email and they have to be signed with an esignature (just a regular signature but digitally) before the event. 

Beds and food are all provided for free but flights aren't automatically covered. Hack Club does offer flight stipends but you'll have to ship a few more hours for them. 
If you're driving, taking a bus, or a train, [Gas](https://gas.hackclub.com/) reimburses you 10 cents per mile traveled. You have to show receipts as proof, though!

Regarding stipends, every event is different. Some hackathons may offer $10/hour but another may offer $8/hour instead. 
The amount given per hour is also affected by how many people are joining and the fees that surround that. 

Find ongoing hackathons here: https://hackathons.hackclub.com/

## Fraud
The Fraud Department performs spot checks on random users to make sure the time tracked in YSWS programs are authentic.
Fraud is defined as "not submitting hours to YSWS programs in good faith, like inflating hours by typing random text instead of actual code."

## History on YSWS events
YSWS events haven't always existed. Hack Club was originally meant to be a place to find workshops to learn and for students to create a "hack club" in their own school to teach about computers and build projects around them.
The first general "any project no matter the theme is accepted" YSWS event was Arcade in 2024.
Since then, several other general YSWS have been hosted including Summer of Making 2025.
Each YSWS is unique in the sense that it each have their own website and unique name and gimick.

In the beginning, #hack-hour was used to track hours for YSWS events, but during Arcade, #hackatime was introduced which used wakatime to intergrate into your IDE to better track your hours and lower fraud by reporting when you were typing and which files were opened and when they were opened.
