The original mission of Hack Club was to make learning fun *in schools* through clubs to spread the excitement of learning.

If you want to start your own club, fill out the form [here](https://hackclub.com/clubs/) to get a care package of stickers and hacker perks like Zoom Pro and Brilliant premium.
Hack Club also offers resources to make your club successful - from ready-to-use workshops to advice from other club leaders and a supportive community.

- Workshops - 100+ workshop lessons to choose from. Simple and digestable: https://workshops.hackclub.com/
- Jams - Collaborative coding workshops where sparks ignite, fears dissolve, and inventions come to life: https://jams.hackclub.com/
- Letter - Letters and write-ups from leaders about their success, how they marketed their club, and the struggles they hit running their own Hack Club club: https://hackclub.com/letter
- A channel in the Slack for club leaders to ask other club leaders for help or ideas: [#leaders](https://hackclub.enterprise.slack.com/archives/C02PA5G01ND)
- Club-only YSWS programs to nab pizza or raspberry pi's for club members
- A monthly newsletter to inform club leaders of what YSWS programs are currently going on
- Hack Club Bank to manage finances when fundraising and such
