Hack Club has a **workspace** on Slack where everything happens. 
You ship we ship programs (I'll explain that more in the next section), activities, Ask-me-anything interviews with prominent figures, Minecraft [insert other things here] (/sic). The Slack is so. Big. There are more channels in the Slack than stars in the sky. Like, any topic you can think of (poetry, hating on windows, loving Linux, drawing, music, whatever else) there's probably a channel for that already. 
Just search with a keyword related to the topic you're interested in using the Slack search bar and join a channel that comes up.

On a different topic: the Slack has a certain...structure (I guess you could call it that). Ysws programs always have a channel for general discussion and a channel to ask for help in. (example: #flavortown for general discussion and #flavortown-help for people to ask for help in). And also a bulletin channel for announcements and the like. 

Slack also uses threads instead of replies. A thread is a mini-channel within a channel.
It's kind of possible to use replies by copying the link to the message and then pasting the link and typing the rest of your message but its not ideal.
Threads are used for long reply chains and the copy-and-paste-message-link trick is used to reply or mention a specific message in the thread (or another thread/channel)

# Moderation
The Slack is moderated by the ~~police department~~ Fire Department (FD for short)!
A bot named Shroud is used to let Hack Clubbers report concerns anonymously (or not) to the Fire Department.
Shroud's code is open-source and on GitHub! (https://github.com/hackclub/shroud)

If you're interested, you can read about how the FD determines which moderation action to perform in a Slack canvas [here](https://hackclub.enterprise.slack.com/docs/T0266FRGM/F07SWGHM90B).
